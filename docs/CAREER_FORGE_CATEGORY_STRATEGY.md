# Career Forge category strategy

Decision date: **2026-07-15**
Selected descriptor: **local-first career evidence compiler**

## Category definition

Career Forge turns scattered, user-approved career evidence into truthful application materials across every credible direction, with visible lineage from source to claim to saved application.

“Compiler” is doing useful work: the user supplies source material, approves the facts, selects intended role lanes, and receives deterministic outputs plus diagnostics. Career Forge is not claiming to invent achievements, predict hiring outcomes, or replace judgment.

The shorter descriptor `career evidence compiler` is appropriate when local-first trust is already visible. Do not rename the product without owner approval.

Alternatives considered:

| Descriptor | Strength | Problem | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| Career Truth System | Memorable and trust-forward | Overclaims objective truth; sounds like surveillance or adjudication | Reject as category name |
| Career Evidence OS | Conveys durable system | “OS” implies a much broader suite | Defer |
| Career Proof Engine | Outcome-oriented | “Proof” can imply credential verification the product does not perform | Reject |
| Career Dossier Compiler | Specific | “Dossier” is less immediately familiar to cold users | Use as explanatory language, not primary descriptor |
| Evidence-backed career operating system | Broad and flexible | Invites the all-in-one suite race | Reject for this stage |

## Target wedge

Primary audience:

> People with nonlinear careers whose strongest evidence is spread across jobs, projects, independent work, old résumés, and real-world responsibilities.

Priority examples are career switchers, operations workers moving into tech-adjacent roles, project-heavy founders, people with strong work but weak titles, recently laid-off workers with several résumé versions, and workers with multiple plausible directions. This is deliberately not “every job seeker.”

## Primary problem

The wedge user does not mainly lack generated prose. They lack a durable, trusted representation of the work they have actually done. Each application forces them to reconstruct that history, choose among conflicting documents, decide what transfers, and trust generated claims they may not be able to defend.

Career Forge’s job is to make that foundation reusable before optimizing or accelerating submission.

## Alternatives and white space

| Alternative | What it does well | What Career Forge must make meaningfully different |
|---|---|---|
| Generic résumé generator | Produces polished copy quickly | Compile only approved evidence and expose why each claim exists |
| ATS/keyword optimizer | Shows document-to-posting similarity and gaps | Distinguish direct, combined, transferred, and missing support without pretending to know hiring probability |
| Job tracker/CRM | Preserves application status and documents | Preserve the evidence and output lineage behind an application |
| Template builder | Formats a résumé attractively | Produce a truthful two-variant baseline pack for every credible role lane |
| Autofill/automation | Reduces submission time | Establish what can be submitted honestly before accelerating anything |
| Human writer | Adds judgment and service | Offer reusable, auditable self-serve compilation for repeated applications |
| Career vault/evidence engine | Stores longitudinal evidence and sometimes provenance | Add a durable pre-trust queue, direct/combined/transferred semantics, simultaneous lane pairs, grounded answers, and local/no-account operation |

The defensible statement is:

> We found no public evidence among the audited products that combines durable pre-trust review, longitudinal evidence, direct/combined/transferred support, duration handling, multi-lane ATS/recruiter packs, evidence-backed answers, complete application lineage, and local no-account use in one workflow.

This is a **combination moat**, not a single-feature moat. ResumeForge and Bragora mean Career Forge must never position “vault,” “truth,” “fact guarding,” or “provenance” alone as unique.

## Product pillars

1. **Truth Inbox** — a durable local queue where imported facts remain proposed until approved; partial review survives navigation and refresh.
2. **Career Dossier** — one approved record across roles, projects, metrics, education, skills, and stories.
3. **Truth Map** — a read-only, bidirectional view from evidence to outputs and from claims back to sources, including unused and stale state.
4. **Role-Lane Pack** — ATS Submission and Recruiter/Networking baselines for every selected credible path.
5. **Defensibility Receipt** — counts and exposes direct, combined, transferred, missing, duration, edit, gap, and actual-refusal state without a probability score.
6. **Application Lineage** — approved evidence → lane → baseline → job analysis → job-specific variant → grounded answers → saved application.
7. **Local-first trust** — no account, local processing, raw imported files not retained, and explicit backup/export.

## Switching logic

| Starting archetype | Why use or switch to Career Forge | When not to switch |
|---|---|---|
| Teal or Huntr user | Build and audit the career foundation once, then use a tracker without losing which facts support which lane/document/application | If the user mainly needs CRM, contacts, or job-board workflow and already trusts their documents |
| Rezi or Jobscan user | Replace another opaque score/rewrite cycle with a receipt that says what is proven, transferable, missing, or deliberately unclaimed | If one document and keyword feedback are the only need |
| Kickresume or Enhancv user | Protect evidence integrity across several directions before selecting final visual presentation | If design variety is the purchase criterion |
| Simplify user | Establish a defensible source of truth before accelerating form completion and high-volume applications | If speed is valued more than review and traceability |
| TopResume buyer | Reuse a self-serve, auditable evidence system across many applications and future career changes | If the user wants a human strategist, writer, or managed search service |
| ResumeForge/Bragora user | Prefer a local, no-account pre-trust review flow with explicit transfer labels, lane pairs, and grounded application answers | If their existing vault already provides those behaviors and cloud convenience matters more |

## Positioning decision

Selected Option A:

> **Your career is bigger than your last résumé.**
>
> Career Forge turns scattered jobs, projects, and old résumés into an approved evidence system, then builds truthful application packs for every credible direction.

Why it won:

- Comprehension: begins with a familiar object and immediately expands beyond it.
- Emotional relevance: recognizes nonlinear careers without labeling the user deficient.
- Uniqueness: naturally supports multi-document import and several role lanes.
- Trust: “approved evidence system” establishes the gate before generation.
- Import intent: explains why several old résumés are valuable.
- Wedge fit: strongest for people whose record is broader than titles.

Option B (`Stop rewriting your career from scratch`) is the best follow-up test for task urgency. Option C (`Résumés generated from proof, not prompts`) is sharp but overstates “proof” and narrows the product to résumés.

Public contrast:

> **Not another AI résumé writer.** Typical tools begin with a document and produce copy, scores, or formatting. Career Forge begins with approved career evidence and shows what supports every baseline, tailored claim, and application answer.

No competitor names should appear in the public comparison without product/legal review.

## Strategic refusal list

Career Forge will not compete on these in this slice:

- hundreds of visual templates
- job-board aggregation
- mass auto-apply or one-click high-volume submission
- speculative hiring predictions
- fake universal ATS scores or opaque “90% match” claims
- social networking features
- a human résumé-writing marketplace
- AI-generated achievements, credentials, dates, or metrics
- keyword stuffing
- gamification detached from evidence or application outcomes
- authentication, cloud sync, subscriptions, or content transmission

The refusal list is a product boundary, not an assertion that these are always bad. It prevents the category from collapsing into an all-in-one suite.

## Success criteria

The category succeeds only when all are true:

1. After a ten-second landing-page exposure, at least 4/5 cold participants independently say it builds a verified/approved career record and traces outputs to evidence—not merely “it makes several résumés.”
2. At least 4/5 can explain why several historical résumés help and why approval is required.
3. All five can find the privacy/no-account explanation and next action.
4. A first-session user can import, partially review, refresh, resume, finish, select a lane, generate a pair, and open lineage without losing work.
5. No proposed/rejected evidence affects readiness, outputs, answers, or analytics activation.

Moderated human testing has **not** occurred. Synthetic acceptance validates mechanics, not comprehension or demand.

## Moat and category risks

- **Fast follower risk:** ResumeForge and Bragora already use overlapping evidence/provenance language. They may expose equivalent workflows behind signup.
- **Combination complexity:** Eleven behaviors are defensible on paper but can be too much for a cold user. Truth Inbox → pack → receipt must remain the visible spine.
- **Review burden:** Approval protects trust but adds work. Batch actions, duplicates, partial saving, and a clear payoff must keep it tolerable.
- **Local durability:** No account is a trust wedge and a loss/sync risk. Backup education is part of the product, not an afterthought.
- **“Compiler” comprehension:** Technical users understand it immediately; broader users may not. Keep the plain-language subhead attached.
- **Proof overclaim:** Source linkage is not third-party verification. Use `approved evidence`, `traced`, and `defensible`; avoid suggesting credential validation.
- **Output quality:** Integrity cannot excuse weak writing or exports. Maintain baseline usefulness without entering a template arms race.
- **Market demand unknown:** Public differentiation and deterministic tests do not prove willingness to adopt or pay.
