# Career Forge market-moat moderated playtest

Status: **owner-ready protocol; not yet conducted**
Minimum sample: **five humans who have not seen Career Forge**

## Participant mix

Recruit at least one person from each profile:

1. A career switcher with experience across two industries.
2. A recently laid-off worker maintaining several résumé files.
3. A project-heavy founder/freelancer whose titles understate the work.
4. An operations or service worker considering a tech-adjacent role.
5. A frequent applicant who currently uses a résumé builder, ATS checker, tracker, or autofill tool.

Do not coach terminology. Use synthetic résumé files only. Do not ask participants to upload private career materials in the research session.

## Ten-second test

Show the landing page at 1440×900 or on the participant’s own device for exactly ten seconds. Hide it and ask:

> What makes this different from an AI résumé generator?

Target concept, not exact wording:

> It builds an approved or verified career record from real history and shows what evidence supports each résumé and application.

Fail the positioning if the response is only “it makes several résumés,” “it uses AI,” or “it scores my résumé.” Record the participant’s exact short answer without suggesting vocabulary.

## Moderated task

1. Return the landing page and ask the participant to begin without instruction.
2. Import two synthetic historical résumés containing an overlap and one contradiction.
3. Approve two facts, reject one, leave at least two undecided, save, refresh, and resume.
4. Finish the review and describe what became trusted.
5. Inspect role lanes and generate one lane’s two-document pack.
6. Open a Defensibility Receipt and follow one claim into the Truth Map.
7. Tailor to a synthetic job, inspect an application-answer source, save the application, then find the lineage.
8. Locate the backup control and explain why it exists.

Do not rescue the participant until they have been stuck for 30 seconds. Record time, misclicks, terminology questions, trust concerns, and any point where proposed evidence is mistaken for approved evidence.

## Required comprehension questions

Ask in this order after the task:

1. What does Career Forge do?
2. Why does it ask you to approve facts?
3. Why would you import more than one résumé?
4. What is a role lane?
5. Why are there two baseline résumés?
6. What does direct versus transferred mean?
7. What happens when Career Forge cannot support a claim?
8. Where is your data stored?
9. What should you do after generating the pack?
10. How is this different from a normal AI résumé builder?

## Scorecard

| Measure | Pass threshold |
|---|---|
| Ten-second uniqueness | 4/5 mention approved/verified evidence and traceability; no more than 1/5 says only “multiple résumés” |
| Approval-gate comprehension | 5/5 know proposed facts cannot support outputs |
| Multi-document intent | 4/5 explain history consolidation, not simply “more keywords” |
| Role-lane comprehension | 4/5 describe a credible career direction |
| Variant comprehension | 4/5 choose ATS for portal and recruiter/networking for human outreach |
| Transfer honesty | 4/5 distinguish related evidence from direct prior experience |
| Unsupported claims | 5/5 understand gaps remain unclaimed/refused, not invented |
| Privacy | 5/5 identify local/no-account storage and need for backup |
| Next step | 4/5 choose tailoring to a real posting |
| End-to-end completion | 4/5 complete with no moderator rescue before the 30-second threshold |

## Recording template

For each participant record: profile, device/viewport, ten-second verbatim answer, first CTA, time to import, time to partial save, refresh recovery success, time to finished review, time to pack, claim-source navigation success, privacy answer, next action, top confusion, top trust signal, and whether they would replace/complement their current tool.

Separate observation from inference. Do not report the five-person result until all sessions occur. Automated personas and browser acceptance validate mechanics only and must not be counted as humans.
