# Career Reset Founding Cohort Operations

This playbook runs the first five paid Career Reset users without pretending the wider pricing model is validated. Personal applicant records, résumé content, and interview notes stay outside this repository.

## Offer boundary

- **Seats:** 5
- **Price:** $49 once
- **Product scope:** one active lane and the Career Reset Pack
- **Added founding support:** guided onboarding, priority friction support, and direct feedback access
- **Not included:** the $149 done-for-you résumé rebuild, unlimited founder labor, guaranteed interviews, or the unopened $79/$99 tiers
- **Completion window:** seven days after acceptance unless another date is agreed in writing

A participant is paying for the Career Reset product outcome and founding-cohort support. The public beta may remain open while automated checkout is finalized; never imply that payment buys exclusive access to otherwise free beta features.

## Applicant inbox

Applications arrive with the subject:

`Career Forge founding cohort — application`

Gmail label:

`Career Forge Founding Cohort`

The daily cohort review should summarize the applicant's background, target direction, urgency, seven-day availability, and any sensitive handling needs.

## Fit rubric

Score each dimension 0–2. Accept strongest-fit applicants until five seats are filled.

| Dimension | 0 | 1 | 2 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Real search | Browsing only | Search planned | Actively applying or restarting now |
| One-lane clarity | No direction | Several possibilities | One credible direction to test first |
| Completion | Cannot commit | Uncertain | Can complete within seven days |
| Real-world use | No planned use | May use outputs | Will use at least one artifact in a real application |
| Feedback | Unavailable | General feedback | Will report friction and outcome honestly |

A score of 8–10 is a strong fit. A lower score may still qualify when the user's situation exposes a strategically important workflow, but record why.

## Payment handoff

Preferred path after checkout configuration:

1. Send the Career Reset checkout URL.
2. Confirm Stripe payment completion.
3. Confirm the license activates and the participant can export.

Temporary founding-cohort path while automated checkout is still being finalized:

1. Use a secure Stripe-hosted payment link or invoice created in the owner's Stripe dashboard.
2. State clearly that the $49 covers the Career Reset founding cohort and guided support while the public beta remains open.
3. Do not collect card details by email, form, chat, or manually.
4. Record only payment status and the Stripe reference outside this repository.
5. Send the onboarding instructions after payment.

Do not accept payment until Stripe live payments and payouts are active and the buyer can receive a receipt from a monitored account.

## Onboarding message

**Subject:** Career Forge onboarding — start here

Hi [Name],

Your founding seat is active. Start here:

1. Open Career Forge: https://career-forge-lite.vercel.app
2. Import one or more existing résumés, or describe one real role/project manually.
3. Review the Truth Inbox. Approve only facts you can defend and reject anything that is wrong, uncertain, or context-only.
4. Choose one target lane.
5. Generate the Career Reset pack.
6. Review the ATS and recruiter drafts, evidence receipt, LinkedIn drafts, and exported files.
7. Use at least one artifact in a real application after checking every claim, date, company, and layout.

Please keep a rough note of how long the workflow takes and which artifacts require light, heavy, or no editing. Do not email résumé content unless you explicitly want founder review. The app stores career data locally in your browser, so download a backup from Settings before clearing browser data or changing devices.

Reply when you reach your first export or whenever a blocker stops you.

Blake

## Completion interview

Ask these after the participant completes the workflow:

1. What did you expect Career Forge to do before you started?
2. Where did you first feel the product understood your history?
3. Which step caused the most hesitation or editing?
4. Which artifact was ready, lightly edited, heavily edited, or abandoned?
5. Did the evidence review catch anything you would not want sent to an employer?
6. Did you use an artifact in a real application?
7. Was the result worth $49 after using it? Why or why not?
8. Would you have requested a refund? What would have caused that?
9. What would make you use Career Forge again for a second direction?

Ask willingness to pay openly before mentioning future prices.

## Seat tracking

Keep a private cohort tracker with:

- Applicant name and email
- Fit score and acceptance reason
- Status: applied / accepted / paid / onboarding / exported / real application / follow-up complete / refunded
- Payment reference
- Start and completion dates
- Editing burden by artifact
- Usefulness rating
- Real application use
- Refund status and cause

Never commit that tracker or participant content to GitHub.

## Cohort close

When five participants have paid:

1. Change the public page to waitlist-only.
2. Keep the $79 and $99 tiers closed.
3. Complete every participant's seven-day and two-week follow-up.
4. Aggregate results without names or résumé content.
5. Compare the cohort against the support threshold in `docs/FOUNDING_USER_PILOT.md` before calling $49 validated.
